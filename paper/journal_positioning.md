# Target journal and positioning

## Recommendation: *Soft Matter* (Royal Society of Chemistry), full paper

## Rationale

1. **Scope match.** Soft Matter's stated scope explicitly covers gels and
   networks, biological aspects of soft matter, and welcomes experimental,
   theoretical, and computational approaches with a particular focus on the
   interface between physics, chemistry, materials science, and biology —
   exactly the disciplinary mix of this manuscript (rigidity-percolation
   physics applied to a biomaterials/wound-healing design question).

2. **Direct benchmark against a paper published in the same journal.** The
   mean-field reverse-gelation constitutive model this work benchmarks
   against — Akalp, Bryant & Vernerey, *Soft Matter* **12**, 7505 (2016) — was
   itself published in *Soft Matter*. A matched-condition, same-trajectory
   quantitative test showing that model diverges from a network-resolved
   ground truth by up to ~172× in the failure regime is a natural and highly
   relevant contribution to the same venue, and the readership is already
   primed to evaluate the comparison.

3. **Established precedent for rigidity-percolation / elastic-network
   computational papers in this journal and its immediate neighbors** (e.g.
   Broedersz & MacKintosh's constraint-counting and biopolymer-network work,
   and multiple rigidity-percolation-in-gels papers appearing in *Soft
   Matter* and *Phys. Rev. E*/*Phys. Rev. X* in the same community) — the
   methodology (elastic-network solver + percolation-threshold measurement +
   design-map sweep) is a recognized genre in this venue rather than a
   novel format that would need extra framing.

4. **Format.** Full paper, not Communication — the manuscript reports six
   figures spanning solver validation, finite-size threshold/exponent
   scanning, coupled dynamics, a design-map sweep, and a literature
   comparison, which exceeds the scope appropriate for a Communication-length
   submission.

## Alternative venues considered

- **Physical Review E** — appropriate for the pure rigidity-percolation
  physics (thresholds, exponent, finite-size scaling) but a weaker fit for
  the applied wound-healing/tissue-engineering framing and the direct
  mean-field-model benchmark, which is the paper's most novel quantitative
  result.
- **Acta Biomaterialia / Journal of the Mechanical Behavior of Biomedical
  Materials** — stronger biomaterials-application framing but a weaker fit
  for the percolation-theory / critical-exponent content, which would likely
  need to be compressed or moved to supplementary material, diluting the
  paper's central physical claim.
- **npj Soft Matter / open-access soft-matter venues** — viable backup if
  *Soft Matter* editors judge the balance too far toward biomedical
  application; noted as a secondary option.

## Note on currency of this positioning

This assessment should be reconfirmed against the venue's current scope and
recent editorial decisions immediately before submission, since new
literature in this actively developing area (e.g. graph-neural-network
approaches to rigidity/connectivity percolation classification, noted in
`NOVELTY.md` as identified during the prior-art search) continues to appear
and could shift the competitive landscape or the appropriate framing.
